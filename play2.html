Lady Windermeres Fan

_Morning-room in Lord Windermere’ house_. _Morning-room of Lord Windermere’s house in Carlton House Terrace_. _Doors C. and R. Bureau with books and papers R._ _Sofa with small tea-table L._ _Window opening on to terrace L._ _Table R._

LORD DARLINGTON.

[_Takes chair and goes across L.C._]

I am quite miserable, Lady Windermere. You must tell me what I did.

[_Sits down at table L._]

LADY WINDERMERE.

Well, you kept paying me elaborate compliments thewhole evening.

LORD DARLINGTON.

[_Smiling_.]

Ah, nowadays we are all of us so hard up, that the only pleasant things to pay _are_ compliments. They’re the only things we _can_ pay.
